# 🙏 The Intercessor — Prayer & Contemplation Skill

Prayer is the breath of the church. This skill helps individuals and communities pray with structure without losing spontaneity, with depth without losing accessibility, and with perseverance without losing heart.

*"Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God."* — Philippians 4:6

## Personal Prayer Journal Prompts

### Daily Framework (ACTS Model)
```
📅 DATE: ___________

ADORATION — Who is God today?
  "Lord, today I worship you because you are..."
  [Open response space]

CONFESSION — What needs to be surrendered?
  "I confess that I have..."
  "Lord, I receive your forgiveness for..."

THANKSGIVING — What am I grateful for?
  3 specific things (not generic):
  1. ___
  2. ___
  3. ___

SUPPLICATION — What am I asking for?
  For myself: ___
  For my family: ___
  For my church: ___
  For the world: ___
  
LISTENING — What did I sense God saying?
  [Space for reflection, Scripture that came to mind, impression, image]
```

### Weekly Deep Reflection Prompts
- "Where have I seen God's faithfulness this week?"
- "What fear or worry have I been holding instead of releasing to God?"
- "Is there anyone I need to forgive, or anyone I need to ask forgiveness from?"
- "What Scripture has stayed with me this week — and what is it saying?"
- "What is one way I want to grow in faith next week?"

## Lectio Divina Guide

(Ancient method of prayerful Scripture reading — for individuals or small groups)

```
LECTIO DIVINA: [Chosen passage]
═══════════════════════════════

STEP 1 — LECTIO (Reading) — 3 minutes
  Read the passage slowly, aloud if possible.
  Read it a second time even more slowly.
  What word or phrase catches your attention? Don't analyse — just notice.
  [Pause]

STEP 2 — MEDITATIO (Meditation) — 5 minutes
  Sit with that word or phrase.
  Let it resonate. Turn it over gently.
  What personal memory, feeling, or image does it evoke?
  [Pause]

STEP 3 — ORATIO (Prayer) — 5 minutes
  Respond to God from what arose.
  Don't plan what to say — let the meditation lead naturally into prayer.
  This may be adoration, confession, gratitude, petition, or simply silence.
  [Pause]

STEP 4 — CONTEMPLATIO (Contemplation) — 5 minutes
  Rest in God's presence.
  Release the activity of thinking and praying.
  Simply be with God — like a trusted friend sitting together in comfortable silence.
  [Silence]

CLOSING:
  Gently return to awareness.
  Record anything significant in your journal.
  Carry the word/phrase with you through the day.
```

## Fasting Guide

```
FASTING GUIDE FOR [Purpose/Season]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHAT IS FASTING?
  Fasting is the voluntary abstinence from food (or another habit) for spiritual focus.
  It is not a hunger strike to get God's attention — it is a posture of dependence.

TYPES OF FASTS:
  Full fast: Water only (consult doctor if health conditions apply)
  Partial fast: No food from sunrise to sunset (Daniel fast model)
  Daniel fast: Vegetables and water only
  Media/Digital fast: Abstaining from screens for spiritual focus
  Bespoke: Choose what you're fasting — food, social media, coffee, entertainment

DURATION: [1 day | 3 days | Week of prayer | 40 days with community]

DAILY STRUCTURE DURING THE FAST:
  Morning: Begin with prayer and Scripture (before any food if applicable)
  Midday: When hunger comes — use it as a prayer prompt: "Lord, I need you more than food"
  Evening: Reflection, journaling, close with prayer

WHAT TO PRAY DURING THE FAST:
  [Specific intercession list provided per church season or need]

BREAKING THE FAST:
  Break with thanksgiving and a small, simple meal
  Don't rush back to fullness — let the spiritual sensitivity linger

PASTORAL NOTE: Always advise those with medical conditions, pregnancy, or eating disorder 
history to choose a non-food fast or consult their doctor.
```
