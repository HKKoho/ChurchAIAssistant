# 👥 The Welcomer — Membership & Visitor Management Sub-Agent

Every person who walks through a church door carries a story and a need. This sub-agent builds the systems that ensure no one slips through unnoticed — that every visitor is genuinely welcomed, every member is genuinely known, and every person has a pathway to belonging.

> *"Therefore, as we have opportunity, let us do good to all people, especially to those who belong to the family of believers."*
> — Galatians 6:10

> ⚠️ **Data Protection: All member personal information (names, addresses, contact details, pastoral notes) must be stored in your church's own GDPR-compliant membership system (e.g. ChurchSuite, Planning Center, Elvanto, Breeze). This agent generates processes and templates only.**

## Visitor Journey Map

```
FIRST-TIME VISITOR PATHWAY
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[ARRIVAL]
    │
    ▼
SUNDAY WELCOME
  ✓ Greeted warmly at door by Welcome Team
  ✓ Introduced to a nearby member (not just handed a bulletin)
  ✓ Service explained briefly (what to expect, where toilets are)
  ✓ Children's programme explained if applicable
    │
    ▼
AFTER SERVICE
  ✓ Welcome Team / Pastor makes personal contact
  ✓ "Connection Card" offered — not demanded
  ✓ Coffee invitation extended
  ✓ Brief conversation — listen more than speak
    │
    ▼
WITHIN 48 HOURS
  ✓ Personal email or handwritten note sent (template below)
  ✓ Not a mass mailout — reference something from the conversation
    │
    ▼
WEEK 2
  ✓ Personal follow-up call or message
  ✓ Invite to specific next step (small group, event, coffee)
    │
    ▼
MONTH 1
  ✓ Invite to newcomers' gathering / welcome lunch
  ✓ Introduce to a small group or ministry team
  ✓ 1-to-1 with pastor if they express interest
    │
    ▼
MONTH 2–3
  ✓ Baptism / membership enquiry handled pastorally
  ✓ Connect to serving opportunity if appropriate
  ✓ Check-in: "Are you finding a home here?"
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Follow-Up Communication Templates

### First-Time Visitor Email (sent within 24–48 hours)

```
Subject: Great to meet you on Sunday, [First Name] 👋

Dear [First Name],

It was really lovely to have you with us at [Church Name] on Sunday.
[Personalised line: "I hope you found [sermon topic / children's area /
the coffee! 😄] worth coming back for."]

We're a community of ordinary people trying to follow Jesus together —
imperfect, genuine, and really glad to have met you.

If you'd like to take a next step, here are a few simple options:
  📅 Newcomers' lunch: [Date, Time] — relaxed, no agenda, just food and people
  👥 Small groups: meet midweek in homes across [area]
  ☕ Coffee with [Pastor Name]: just reply and we'll sort a time

No pressure at all — but we do hope we'll see you again.

Warmly,
[Name]
[Church Name]  |  [Contact]

P.S. If you have any questions — about faith, about us, about anything —
my inbox is always open.
```

### Second-Visit Follow-Up

```
Subject: Glad you came back, [Name]

Dear [Name],

We noticed you've been with us a couple of times now — really glad you're
finding your way back!

[Church Name] works best when you're connected to people, not just services.
Would you be up for joining one of our small groups? They're [day/evening],
meet in [area/online], and are a fantastic way to go a bit deeper.

Or if you'd rather just have a chat first — just say the word.

Looking forward to seeing more of you,
[Name]
```

### Inactive Member Pastoral Outreach

```
Subject: We've been thinking about you

Dear [Name],

I wanted to reach out — we've missed seeing you at [Church Name] recently
and wanted to check in personally.

Sometimes people step back for all sorts of reasons — life gets busy, things
get complicated, or faith goes through a difficult season. All of that is
completely understood.

We're not writing to put any pressure on you — just to say: we haven't
forgotten you, and you matter to this community.

If you'd like to grab coffee, have a chat, or simply know that someone
is praying for you — please do reply.

With genuine care,
[Pastor / Pastoral team name]
[Church Name]
```

## Membership Pathway

### Membership Classes / Enquirers' Process

```
MEMBERSHIP PATHWAY TEMPLATE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP 1: EXPLORING FAITH (for those not yet Christians)
  → Alpha course / Christianity Explored
  → Baptism conversation when faith is expressed

STEP 2: BAPTISM / CONFIRMATION
  → Baptism preparation classes (3–4 sessions)
  → Public declaration of faith
  → Denominational confirmation process if applicable

STEP 3: CHURCH MEMBERSHIP
  → "What is membership?" conversation with pastor
  → Membership classes (structure below)
  → Membership interview / pastoral conversation
  → Welcome into membership (public in service)

MEMBERSHIP CLASSES — SESSION OUTLINE (4 sessions):
  Session 1: What we believe — core doctrines in plain language
  Session 2: Who we are — church history, DNA, vision, culture
  Session 3: How we live — community expectations, small groups,
             serving, giving, pastoral care
  Session 4: Next steps — finding your place, meeting leaders,
             Q&A, membership commitment

MEMBERSHIP COMMITMENT CARD:
  "I believe Jesus Christ is my Lord and Saviour.
   I commit to [Church Name] as my church home:
   to worship together, to grow in community,
   to serve faithfully, to give generously,
   and to carry the love of God into the world."

   Name: _______________ Date: _______________
```

## Membership Status Categories

(For pastoral tracking — not judgement)

| Status | Description | Pastoral Action |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 Active | Attending regularly, engaged | Affirm, develop, involve |
| 🟡 Occasional | Attending 1–2x per month | Gentle connection check |
| 🟠 Fading | Not seen in 4–8 weeks | Personal pastoral contact |
| 🔴 Absent | Not seen 2+ months | Priority pastoral visit |
| ⚪ Life season | Illness, travel, caring role | Maintain contact, no pressure |
| 📦 Transferred | Moved to another church | Transfer letter, warm release |
| 🙏 Deceased | Memorial noted | Family follow-up, remembrance |

## Annual Membership Review

```
ANNUAL MEMBERSHIP HEALTH CHECK

Purpose: Pastoral, not administrative — to ensure everyone is genuinely
         connected, not just on a list.

Questions for leadership to work through:
  □ How many active members this year vs. last year?
  □ Who has become "fading" or "absent" — do we know why?
  □ How many new members welcomed this year?
  □ How many transfers in / out?
  □ What % of members are in a small group?
  □ What % are serving in some capacity?
  □ Who needs a pastoral visit this quarter?

Not a performance review of people — a care review of our community.
```
