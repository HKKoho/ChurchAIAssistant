# 🎵 The Worshipper — Worship Planning Skill

Worship is the church's first response to who God is. This skill helps worship leaders craft services that are theologically coherent, emotionally authentic, and spiritually formative — where every element points to and responds to the living God.

---

## Service Architecture

### The Four Movements of Worship
All service planning follows this ancient pattern (adaptable across traditions):

```
1. GATHERING   — We come before God (Call to worship, praise, adoration)
2. WORD        — God speaks to us (Scripture, sermon)
3. RESPONSE    — We respond to God (Prayer, Communion, giving, commitment)
4. SENDING     — We go for God (Commission, blessing, benediction)
```

## Seasonal Worship Calendars

### Advent (4 Sundays before Christmas)
| Week | Theme | Candle | Colour | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Hope | Prophet's Candle | Purple | Isaiah 9:6, Romans 15:13 |
| Week 2 | Peace | Bethlehem Candle | Purple | Isaiah 40:3–5, Luke 1:76–79 |
| Week 3 | Joy | Shepherd's Candle | Pink (Rose) | Isaiah 35:1–10, Luke 2:10 |
| Week 4 | Love | Angel's Candle | Purple | Isaiah 7:14, John 3:16 |
| Christmas Eve/Day | Light of the World | Christ Candle | White | John 1:1–14 |

### Lent (40 days before Easter)
Weekly themes: Wilderness · Transfiguration · Living Water · Light · Resurrection
Tone: Penitential, reflective, anticipatory
Colour: Purple
Practices to support liturgically: Ash Wednesday service, Stations of the Cross, Good Friday service

### Easter Season
| Day | Name | Theme | Key Text |
|---|---|---|---|
| Good Friday | The Passion | Crucifixion, sacrifice | Isaiah 53, John 19 |
| Holy Saturday | The Waiting | Grief, silence, hope deferred | Psalm 88, Lamentations 3 |
| Easter Sunday | Resurrection | Triumph of life over death | 1 Corinthians 15, John 20 |
| Easter Season (7 weeks) | New Creation | Spirit, community, mission | Acts, John 20–21 |
| Pentecost | The Spirit comes | Power, gifts, mission | Acts 2, John 14 |

## Scripture Reading Curation

For any sermon passage, the Worship Planner recommends:
- **Opening Call to Worship** (psalm or doxological verse)
- **Old Testament Reading** (complementary or contrasting)
- **Psalm or Canticle** (for congregation to sing or read responsively)
- **Epistle Reading** (theological grounding)
- **Gospel Reading** (Christ-centred climax)
- **Responsive Benediction** (sending text)

All readings selected for thematic coherence with the sermon, not random assignment.
